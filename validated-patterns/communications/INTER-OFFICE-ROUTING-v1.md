## Pattern

Cross-office routing should be explicit and role-based.

Default routing logic inherited from the predecessor shell:

- mechanical execution or validation -> adjudicator
- corpus survey or topology sensing -> cartographer
- cohesion, substrate calibration, or automation -> psyche
- strategic framing or high-level sensing -> ajna
- federal orchestration or escalation -> commander

## Why It Survives

The predecessor shell correctly understood that ambiguity about who should take the next step creates more hidden work than almost any tooling problem.

Role-based routing is useful even when the exact model or harness changes.

## Successor-Shell Translation

This pattern should not be treated as immutable personnel law. It is a routing default:

- offices may change harnesses
- machines may move
- model capabilities may shift

What should survive is the decision logic, not every historical platform choice.

## Required Cross-Office Metadata

Every cross-office handoff should include:

- task or line identifier
- current decision envelope
- expected receipt
- completion or acknowledgement condition

## Compaction Targets

This pattern should eventually inform:

- handoff templates
- office-local dispatch defaults
- routing operators and queue selection

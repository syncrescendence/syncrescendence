## Pattern

Every office and major line of work should support two review cadences:

1. **directive-close critique**
   - verify receipts
   - check translation quality
   - log anti-patterns
   - propose one improvement

2. **weekly evaluation**
   - review recently completed work
   - score repeatable invariants
   - decide whether a line remains manual, should be white-labeled, or should be verticalized

## Why It Survives

The predecessor shell was right about one important thing: repeated work that is never scored or critiqued remains artisanal forever.

This pattern converts that intuition into successor-shell quality law:

- local work should improve future work
- repeated friction should become a pattern or operator
- weak lines should be downgraded before they create more complexity debt

## Successor-Shell Translation

The original three-track rubric should not be copied blindly, but its structure is useful:

- objective fidelity
- translation quality
- receipt quality
- continuation quality
- repository sovereignty
- scalability / verticalization readiness

These dimensions belong in successor-shell quality management and operator selection.

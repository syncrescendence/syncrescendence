# Dissertation Chapter 5 — Open Questions, Risks, and Agent Habitation

## 1. Open Questions

The shell is converging, but a few questions still matter enough that they must remain explicit:

### How compact should live Rosetta be?

Too small and it loses originality.
Too large and it becomes a semantic gravity well again.

### How strong should executive law be?

Too weak and the shell loses direction.
Too strong and local experimentation and differentiated playbooks get stifled.

### How much local office memory is healthy?

Too little and the offices become sterile and forgetful.
Too much and they become hidden state silos.

### How far should chat surfaces go?

Too little and handoff friction remains unnecessarily high.
Too much and Slack/Discord/Telegram become pseudo-canons.

### How should connector verification authority be partitioned?

Too centralized and verification becomes a human bottleneck.
Too distributed and connector state quality degrades into untrusted claims.

### What is the endgame of worker fabric?

Is the dominant future:

- browser workers
- subscription-worker fabric
- chat-bus-mediated coordination
- stronger local operators
- or a hybrid of all of these?

The shell likely wants the hybrid, but the mature division of labor is not final yet.

### How far should the imported strategic and taxonomic architecture be physically translated?

The imported strategic-vision and operational-taxonomy pair are rich and directionally correct.
But they are not a command to rewrite the successor shell literally.

The open question is:

- which parts should become live shell law
- which parts should remain translation doctrine
- which parts should wait until substantive pipelines exist to justify them

## 2. Risks

The shell’s main risks are no longer “lack of ideas.” They are architectural regressions.

### Risk 1 — constitutional sprawl

If too much teleology, modality, and doctrine are stuffed back into one giant root slab, the shell recreates the old overload problem.

### Risk 2 — hidden local authority

If offices, chat surfaces, or runtime states become where “the real truth” lives, repo sovereignty collapses.

### Risk 3 — compaction failure

If repeated work is never promoted into playbooks, operators, or validated patterns, the shell reverts to artisanal heroics.

### Risk 4 — semantic overproduction

The predecessor Rosetta proved that originality without sufficient tiering can become debt.

### Risk 5 — topology relapse

The old shell’s bad topology is attractive because it is convenient.
Duplicate logistics, mixed artifact classes, and ad hoc root lanes can quietly return.

### Risk 6 — verification theater

If connector verification stays at narrative level rather than receipt-backed state transitions, the shell can appear governed while remaining operationally blind at the exact integration edges where failures happen.

### Risk 7 — governance recursion

If the shell keeps elaborating family law, control-plane law, and projection law without spending enough energy on substantive intelligence pipelines, it can become beautifully governed and strategically underproductive.

This is a real risk now.
The architecture has matured enough that the next failure mode is not chaos.
It is over-governed latency.

## 3. What Agents Need in Order to Move In

Agents do not just need files.
They need a livable institution.

That means they need:

### clear office identity

They need to know:

- who they are
- what burden they carry
- where they stop
- what is not theirs to hold

### clear artifact law

They need to know:

- what kind of artifact they are producing
- where it belongs
- what makes it durable
- what is only local

### clear promotion paths

They need to know how local work becomes:

- communication
- runtime evidence
- playbook doctrine
- validated pattern
- executive or program delta

### clear modality

They need root-level and harness-level cues that explain their proper mode:

- execution
- review
- survey
- strategy
- cohesion

### protection from condemned habits

They need the shell to make bad structure harder than good structure.

## 4. What a Habitable Shell Feels Like

If the shell is working, an agent should experience:

- clear entry
- clear law
- clear local office
- clear routing
- clear closure
- clear compaction destination

It should not feel like:

- reading the whole archaeology to do basic work
- guessing where artifacts belong
- inventing handoffs from scratch
- leaving important state only in the session

## 5. The Grand Vision in Operational Terms

The grand vision is not merely:

- more agents
- more tools
- more automation

It is:

**a sovereign shell that can absorb increasing capability while preserving clarity, originality, and institutional memory.**

Operationally, that means:

- multiple differentiated offices
- one constitutional source
- one lawful truth plane
- many execution surfaces
- governed exocortex
- operatorized compaction
- ontology as registry
- continuous molting rather than episodic collapse
- real intelligence pipelines that justify the governance stack

## 6. Final Claim

The successor shell is ready for habitation in principle, but still in staged occupancy.

The right stance now is:

- move agents in carefully
- keep proving the shell with real lines of work
- compact aggressively
- preserve pedigree
- refuse topology relapse
- require evidence-backed verification transitions on high-risk integration surfaces
- pivot mainline effort toward the Acumen intelligence pipeline and other substantive intelligence-production lines once governance residue becomes bounded

If that discipline holds, this shell can become the first genuinely durable Syncrescendent form rather than just the latest reorganization.

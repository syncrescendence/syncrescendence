**Status**: ratified campaign synthesis

**Purpose**: collapse Campaign 05 into one macroscopic reading of what became operational and what remains seed-state

## 1. Executive Finding

Campaign 05 widened the shell from typed law into the first operational family infrastructure.

It did this in four distinct ways:

1. office-harness state became validator-backed, reportable, and multi-office
2. tributary disposition completed its non-breaking authority transition
3. config-surface state became machine-addressable as a real seed registry and projection matrix
4. the live-ledger family register and widening gates became shared control surfaces

This is the first campaign where the shell starts to look like a typed internal operating system rather than a law corpus plus receipts.

## 3. Holistic Evaluation

Campaign 05 is a significant acceleration step because it moved multiple families from doctrine into machine-addressable state simultaneously.

### 3.1 Office-harness is now a real family, but not yet a full operative family

`commander` is operative.
The other four offices are still `reference-specimen` and therefore `informative_only`.

That means the family exists, validates, and reports cleanly, but it still lacks:

- full multi-office operative promotion
- append-only rebinding history
- a rematerialization law from event ledger into effective registry

### 3.2 Tributary disposition is now the most mature live proof family in the shell

The tributary control plane is now the clearest example of the full transition path:

- law
- registry
- ledger
- validator
- compatibility receipt
- report surface

It remains the shell's strongest already-live family proof.

### 3.3 Config-surface state is real, but still pre-operational

The config kernel is now machine-addressable, but not yet executable as a family.

Missing:

- append-only config substrate
- drift or receipt ledger
- a validator or operator that consumes the seed registry
- a first repo-native `chat_ci` render or receipt surface

### 3.4 Live-ledger widening now has a shared gate language

This is important because it prevents seed-state surfaces from being mistaken for live families.

The register correctly distinguishes:

- `tributary_disposition` as `phase1_repo_proof`
- `office_harness_state` as `phase0_lawful_seed`
- `config_surface_state` as `phase0_lawful_seed`

That is a healthy macroscopic sign: the shell is beginning to know not just what exists, but what maturity phase each family is in.

## 4. What Remains Partial

Campaign 05 did not yet finish the next infrastructural turn.

Still missing:

- `office-harness-binding-ledger.jsonl`
- live rematerialization rule from office-harness ledger to effective registry
- operative promotion of the remaining four federal office bindings
- richer validator enforcement or normalization for runtime-heavy OpenClaw fields
- config-surface append-only receipt or drift ledger
- a validator or operator that reads the config-surface seed
- a first repo-native `chat_ci` render or receipt surface
- `live-ledger-domain-register-ledger.jsonl`

These are not local cleanups.
They are the next macro infrastructure step.

## 5. The Next Macroscopic Leg

The next macroscopic leg should be:

`seed-state families -> repo-native append-only substrate -> deterministic rematerialization -> phase1 repo proof -> exocortex-facing widening`

That is the correct acceleration vector because the shell now has enough law, typing, and seed state.
Its next bottleneck is not more explanation.
Its next bottleneck is converting seed-state families into true repo-native proof families that can widen into exocortex surfaces without semantic drift.

The first three families to drive through that leg are:

1. office-harness state
2. config-surface state
3. live-ledger domain register itself

Only after those gain append-only mutation history and deterministic rematerialization should exocortex projections widen materially.

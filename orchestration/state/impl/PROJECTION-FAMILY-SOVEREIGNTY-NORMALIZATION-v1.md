# Projection Family Sovereignty Normalization v1

**Status**: live-v1
**Class**: implementation law
**Purpose**: normalize how derivative projection families declare capture posture, allowed reliance, and sovereignty boundaries

## Compact Rule

- repo ratifies source meaning and allowed widening
- exocortex coordinates only where a ratified family contract permits it
- projection artifacts remain derivative, replaceable, and subordinate

No derivative projection family may become a hidden second control plane.

## Required Governance Envelope

Any current-state or projection-ready derivative artifact that widens beyond a purely local source file should carry a `projection_governance` object.

Minimum fields:

- `shared_contract_path`
- `sovereignty_rule`
- `capture_policy`
- `reliance_ceiling`
- `hidden_second_control_plane`

Required values:

- `shared_contract_path: orchestration/state/impl/PROJECTION-FAMILY-SOVEREIGNTY-NORMALIZATION-v1.md`
- `sovereignty_rule: repo_ratifies_exocortex_coordinates_projection_derives`
- `hidden_second_control_plane: prohibited`

## Field Meanings

### `capture_policy`

This field names how the derivative artifact may lawfully leave or mirror its repo-native source.

Allowed v1 values:

- `repo_join_snapshot`: lawful only for repo-built current-state joins that remain rebuildable from ratified repo-native proof surfaces
- `manual_export_only`: lawful only for repo-native projection-ready artifacts that may inform a human copy or export step but may not automate provider sync or vendor-side mutation

### `reliance_ceiling`

This field names the strongest reliance the artifact may claim.

Allowed v1 values:

- `row_scoped_contract_bound`: row-level reliance may rise only where a family contract and upstream proof state explicitly allow it
- `informative_only_until_receipted_export`: the artifact is planning or packing guidance only until a narrower receipted export family is ratified

## Family Normalization Rule

1. A derivative family must name one lawful `capture_policy`; silence is not acceptable once widening posture matters.
2. A derivative family must name one `reliance_ceiling`; it may not imply stronger authority through convenience wording.
3. Projection artifacts may repeat upstream ratification and coordination facts, but they may not replace them.
4. Manual-export families must stay human-mediated and explicitly non-automated until a narrower repo-ratified receipt family exists.
5. Repo-joined current-state families must remain rebuildable from repo-native proof state without hidden caches or vendor APIs.
6. If an artifact cannot satisfy these fields cleanly, it must remain out of scope rather than create a parallel authority surface.

## Effect

This contract is immediately binding on derivative projection families that:

- join repo-native proof state into outward-facing current-state artifacts
- prepare provider-facing packing or export surfaces
- claim to widen through exocortex or vendor-controlled interfaces

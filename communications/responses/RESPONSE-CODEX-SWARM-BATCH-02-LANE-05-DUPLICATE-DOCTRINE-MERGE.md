## 1. Witness-Precedence Model

1. **Live-shell ratified witness**
   Present-tense authority. If a live artifact already governs the question, it wins on current behavior even if older witnesses are richer.

2. **Neo constitutional witness**
   Default base text when the live shell needs doctrine and no live artifact has yet ratified a fuller treatment. `Neo` is the preferred promotion substrate because it already compresses toward the successor shell.

3. **Old burden-bearing witness**
   Mandatory annex witness when `old` preserves rationale, thresholds, protocols, or operational grain that `neo` compressed away and the loss would change practice.

4. **Pedigree collision witness**
   Required when neither tributary can lawfully swallow the other without destroying scope, topology, or unresolved dissent. In that case the family remains explicitly plural in pedigree/registry rather than silently merged.

## 3. When Dual Witness Must Stay Explicit

Dual witness should remain explicit in the live shell when any of the following are true:

1. `Neo` supplies shell-fit compression but `old` carries a materially different protocol, threshold, or lifecycle rule.
2. The live shell currently uses only a compact proxy term such as repo sovereignty, continuation/deletability, office, or pedigree, while the fuller doctrine still lives upstream.
3. Batch 01 or direct reading already showed depth-loss or flattening in the `neo` treatment, especially in families like memory, feed curation, platform grammar residue, and evaluation.
4. One witness is constitutional and the other is the only preserved annex-level operationalization.
5. Removing one witness would make it impossible to reconstruct why the live shell chose its present form.

Operationally, explicit dual witness means:

- the promoted live artifact cites both witnesses
- the registry uses one `merge_family_id` with multiple source rows
- pedigree or annex surfaces keep the losing-but-still-binding witness inspectable

## 4. When To Record A Collision Instead Of Merging

An unresolved collision should remain recorded when any of these hold:

1. **Topology conflict**: the family assumes incompatible physicalizations, such as five-account IIC states versus the current `offices/` and harness model.
2. **Scope conflict**: one witness is scripture/pedigree and the other is operational law, with no live-shell artifact yet translating between them.
3. **Enforcement conflict**: the witnesses imply different ratios, gates, or termination conditions and the live shell has not ratified one.
4. **Volatility conflict**: old content is too platform-bound or date-bound to merge directly, but too epistemically valuable to cull.
5. **Authority-gap conflict**: the live shell has fragments of the family, but not enough to claim a unified successor doctrine honestly.

When a collision is recorded:

- keep both witnesses in pedigree or reference form
- mark the family as unresolved in the disposition registry rather than silently “merged”
- store the reason in `notes_on_ambiguity`
- prefer `retain_pedigree` or `promote_reference` over premature `promote_live_law`

## 5. Failure Modes If Merge Happens By Style Instead Of Witness Discipline

1. **Compression masquerades as completeness**
   Cleaner `neo` prose gets treated as if it already contains all operational burdens, and the shell loses old thresholds, exceptions, and failure-mode knowledge.

2. **Authenticity gets laundered out**
   Burden-bearing old rationale disappears, so future readers inherit conclusions without the pressure that produced them.

3. **Pedigree becomes fake**
   The system starts claiming a single ancestry where the record actually contains competing witnesses, flattening auditability.

4. **Rejected topology sneaks back in**
   Old account/state or shell-shape assumptions get reintroduced under a stylish merged doctrine instead of being held as explicit historical residue.

5. **Reference law and constitutional law collapse**
   Strong topic material such as feedcraft or scripture gets promoted into live shell law without the shell first deciding whether it is reference, pedigree, or constitution.

6. **Registry and receipts stop being able to explain outcomes**
   If the losing witness is not named, later reviewers cannot reconstruct why a family was merged, thinned, annexed, or left unresolved.

## Net Law

Use this default:

- live shell decides present behavior
- `neo` decides default promotion shape
- `old` decides whether the compressed promotion is honest enough
- if not, keep dual witness explicit
- if still not reconcilable, record the collision instead of synthesizing it away

That is the merge discipline that preserves shell fitness without destroying burden-bearing nuance.

## Source-of-Truth Principles

1. Account identity is constitutional, not script-defined.
   `acumen`, `coherence`, `efficacy`, `mastery`, and `transcendence` are chain roles first. Browser automation, follow scripts, or migration routines are operators over those roles, not the thing being defined.
2. Live platform state is the origin witness, but not enough by itself.
   The actual follow/subscription graph on X, YouTube, or other platforms is the first witness of what an account is currently curating.
3. Captured manifests are the repo-side source of truth for inbound feed state before mutation.
   Once a feed graph is captured, the manifest becomes the durable witness for parity checks, import decisions, and later audits.
4. Imported Acumen portfolio and registry state are the operational source of truth for the current shared cycle.
   A followed source is not automatically part of the live Acumen machine. It enters the shared cycle only after lawful import into the portfolio or registry surface.
5. Downstream products are derivative, never feed authority.
   `Dawn Brief`, verification dossiers, Augur packets, assessments, and primary-source queues may reflect what intake surfaced, but they do not decide account identity or feed membership.
6. Overlap is allowed, but rationale must stay chain-specific.
   The same source may appear in more than one account if the reason differs by chain. A frontier lab might belong in Acumen for monitoring, in Coherence for framework work, and in Efficacy for implementation relevance. That is lawful only when the account-role rationale stays explicit.

## Boundary Between Inbound Curation And Dissemination

This contract governs:

1. who each account curates
2. how that curation is captured before mutation
3. how eligible sources are imported into the Acumen-led cycle
4. how later chains consume intelligence without becoming shadow intake planes

This contract does **not** govern:

1. outbound posting strategy
2. audience growth or engagement tactics
3. mass-follow or mass-unfollow mutation as an end in itself
4. platform automation that treats accounts as interchangeable shells
5. dissemination or projection families downstream of triage, verification, and assessment

The key boundary is simple:

- inbound curation chooses and preserves upstream intelligence substrate
- dissemination distributes already-derived outputs downstream

If those blur, feed identity collapses into platform behavior and the chain ecology loses sovereignty.

## Main Sovereignty And Ambiguity Risks

1. **Acumen intake sovereignty drift**
   If Coherence, Efficacy, Mastery, or Transcendence feeds start landing shared-cycle items directly, Acumen stops being the sovereign intake plane and the current intelligence cycle fractures into hidden parallel authorities.
2. **Account-role collapse into mutation tooling**
   If the contract is implemented as "copy follows from account A to account B" without preserving chain rationale, the result is account cloning, not a lawful constellation.
3. **Source-of-truth ambiguity across four layers**
   Platform follow state, captured manifests, imported registry state, and downstream briefs are different truth classes. If they are treated as interchangeable, audits and feed governance will fail.
4. **False symmetry across the five accounts**
   Canonically all five accounts are real, but operationally the current shared cycle is Acumen-led. Treating all five as equally active intake engines would overstate current reality and create ungoverned complexity.
5. **Role overlap without explicit reason codes**
   Some sources legitimately belong to multiple chain accounts. Without a recorded reason, overlap will look like duplication or drift instead of intentional chain ecology.
6. **Inbound/dissemination boundary failure**
   If feed capture work turns into audience growth, outbound cloning, or posting automation, the upstream intelligence substrate gets confused with downstream projection.
7. **Identity/session ambiguity during capture**
   The feed-recon canon is explicit here: if the operator cannot prove which account is active in a browser session, capture must stop. Otherwise the wrong account can be ratified as source-of-truth.

# Tributary Adjudication v1

**Status**: live secondary doctrine
**Class**: Sigma
**Purpose**: give the shell a compact tribunal for deciding what predecessor or archive material should be preserved, translated, promoted, or culled

## Governing Rule

Tributaries are witnesses, not sovereigns.

Promote durable claims and governing patterns, not prior folder topologies, duplicated scaffolding, or time-bound inventories.

## Adjudication Questions

1. What is the artifact's live burden?
   - principle, secondary doctrine, reference substrate, operator, pedigree, or residue
2. What must survive?
   - essence, rationale, proven procedure, annex detail, or only custody
3. What expires?
   - topology-bound structure, stale inventories, temporal capability claims, duplicated scaffolding
4. What is the honest landing zone?
   - `knowledge/canon/`, `knowledge/sigma/`, `knowledge/sigma/references/`, `validated-patterns/`, `playbooks/`, `operators/`, `pedigree/`, or cull
5. What action best preserves truth?
   - preserve, condense, merge, translate, complete, redesign, demote, evict

## Promotion Gate

Promote only when the family shows:

- gravity: it pulls on multiple later decisions
- durability: its core claim should still matter without constant refresh
- non-redundancy: it adds something not already ratified
- lane fit: its class matches the target surface
- operational yield: the shell will consult it repeatedly

## Tribunal Rules

- preserve witness identity before compaction
- extract principle and release inventory
- prefer fewer, deeper artifacts over many thin restatements
- keep reference substrate separate from live doctrine; cite it instead of absorbing it whole
- send deterministic mechanics to `playbooks/`, `operators/`, or `validated-patterns/`, not Sigma
- send ancestry, authenticity reserve, and rationale reserve to `pedigree/`
- redesign when the insight is real but the inherited structure is wrong

## Minimum Family Receipt

For every tributary family, record:

- family name or id
- base witness
- annex witnesses
- action taken
- surviving claim
- landing target
- residue retained elsewhere
- why the artifact does not belong higher or lower

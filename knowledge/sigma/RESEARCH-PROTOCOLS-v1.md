# Research Protocols v1

**Status**: live secondary doctrine
**Class**: Sigma
**Purpose**: preserve the shell's reusable method for decision-bearing research without overstating it as bind-on-default canon

## Governing Rule

Research exists to change a decision.

If the answer will not change action, confidence requirement, or risk posture, do not spend shell attention on it.

## Protocol

1. Define the decision-bearing question.
   - What decision does this inform?
   - What changes if X versus Y?
   - What confidence is required to act?
   - What is the cost of being wrong?
2. Run the Source Triad.
   - Primary source: original material, or repo/runtime evidence when the claim is about local state
   - High-signal secondary analysis: interpretation that adds consequences, limits, or implementation meaning
   - Counter-position: the strongest credible rebuttal, limitation, or alternative explanation
3. Produce a verdict, not a link dump.
   - claim
   - confidence with rationale
   - recency
   - volatility
   - design delta
4. Handle contradiction explicitly.
   - record both claims
   - grade source quality
   - define the resolution path
   - use the conservative posture while unresolved
5. Stop when the decision clears.
   - research that no longer affects action should be compacted, handed off, or dropped rather than prolonged for volume

## Anti-Patterns

- benchmark shopping
- link dumping
- hype amplification
- recency bias
- confirmation bias

## Minimum Output Contract

Every decision-bearing research artifact should end with:

- the decision at stake
- the triad witnesses consulted
- the verdict
- the design delta
- open contradictions or follow-ups
